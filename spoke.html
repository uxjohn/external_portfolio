THE PROBLEM

IBM employees were talking to the same external clients and giving them differing information about the same product, resulting in confusion and bad client relations. They stakeholders of the project approached us with preconceptions of what they expected the final solution to be. Through research we redefined the clients problem from the users perspective and I managed alignment across stakeholders using Design Thinking techniques.

THE APPROACH

Initially a period of research was undertaken to clarify and define the users needs, experience goals and task goals. We used semi structured interviews with users and analysed each interview as a team to generate “tribal knowledge” that would allow us to define two personas for the project.

THE SOLUTION

This primary and secondary research helped us create the followingdesign principals that guided the design:

  • Hot triggers to write information – Make it incredibly easy to wright information.
  • Passive ability to read information – People find reading boring, however we have an enormous amount of content to generate. Therefore we should allow users to passively consume large amounts of data.
  • Ensure that for each design decision that the user has a trigger a motivation and an ability to improve the chance of the behaviour happening.
  • Give the user a platform to tell their colleagues what they have done (show off) and in turn their colleagues will receive information that will be useful to them.
